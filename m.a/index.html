Kate Wihongi Esq

I am fluent in Māori, Spanish with a collective ten years of experience in immigration law as a paralegal/volunteer intern, and later, as an attorney. I had wanted to become an immigration advocate ever since High School, when I was deeply impacted by an immigration raid I had witnessed. I earned my B.S. in Legal Studies and Spanish Certification at the University of Maryland while volunteering with Casa de Maryland and interning at various immigration firms. I later worked as an immigration paralegal while continuing with my goal of attending law school. In 2016 I graduated from Regent School of Law and in 2017 I became a bar-licensed attorney in Virginia. Over the next several years I practiced as an immigration defense attorney and that experience has included Motions to Reopen prior deportation and removal orders with the Executive Office for Immigration Review, Master and Individual Hearings before the Immigration Court for those seeking relief from removal, motions and hearings before the United State

District Court, review and drafting of petitions presented to the 4th Circuit, Appeals to the Board of Immigration Appeals, Interviews before the Asylum Office, Stays of Removal before Immigration and Customs Enforcement, and benefit applications of all types before the United States Citizenship and Immigration Service. I also have family and criminal law experience insomuch as it relates to immigration cases, such as with Special Immigrant Juvenile Status Petitions or Immigration Bond and Detainee Removal Hearings. I am a recent arrival to the Charlotte, North Carolina area and I am delighted to bring my experience to the immigrant community of Charlotte and surrounding state areas. I play the harp and am a nature lover, who enjoys outdoor activities and going on adventures with my friends and family in my spare time.

Maximus Ututaonga Hohaia Esq

Leading the team is Maximus Hohaia Ututaonga. Maxi as he’s fondly called has over 25 years experience of law. For much of that time Maxi has been on the Lawyer for Child panel and is a senior counsel in that field. Maxi is also on the panel of lawyers who are able to accept appointments from the Central Authority under the Hague Convention and is on the IDCCR panel which means he can accept appointments under the Intellectual Disability (Compulsory Care and Rehabilitation) Act.

In addition to those personal appointments Maxi is also the lawyer/counsel for the Open Home Foundation of New Zealand within the Wider Wellington Region.

Maxi has also been a Youth Advocate in the Youth Court for many years and has considerable experience of representing young people between ages of 14 and 17 in that jurisdiction.

Maxi is extremely passionate about the rights of children and the importance of them having a voice that can be heard within the legal process – whether that be in the Family or Youth Courts.

Maxi also has considerable experience in all relationship property matters and has always been motivated to achieve the best possible outcome for his clients and believes that he has been successful in achieving that. As much as anything else, this includes providing his clients with realistic legal advice in terms of possible outcomes and associated legal costs and not simply telling them what they may wish to hear.

Maxi is married (to the Practice Manager of Reids, Fiona) with three adult children; enjoys a rural lifestyle and keeping fit by swimming and walking their two dogs. Paul is an enthusiastic music lover and enjoys attending live concerts, sports, travelling and also enjoys nothing more than sharing a beer or a good red wine with friends and family.
